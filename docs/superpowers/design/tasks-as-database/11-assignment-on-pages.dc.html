11 · Team + user assignment on Pages — research & combined control
Index ← Anatomy

Today team and user never appear on a card together — one is always implied by context. Pages break that assumption in exactly one case. Findings below are read from the source, not assumed.

What the code does today
Card shows team XOR user. TaskFooter: show​Team = area === USER_DASH — on /me/tasks the user is the viewer, so the card shows the team; on team dashboards and in meetings the team is the context, so the card shows the user.
Selectable teams = the viewer's teams. TaskFooterTeamAssigneeMenuQuery → viewer.teams. NOT the union of people invited to a page — you can never move a task onto a team you aren't a member of (ChangeTaskTeamMutation enforces this).
Selectable users = members of the task's team. TaskFooterUserAssigneeMenuQuery → viewer.team(taskTeamId).teamMembers. Page invitees who aren't on that team cannot be assignees.
Locks. Integrated tasks can't change user (canAssignUser = !integration && !isArchived); archived tasks can't change either.

Jordan's hypothesis holds, narrowed: both are needed only on pages not nested under a team — shared pages with multi-team invitees, and private pages when the viewer is on 2+ teams (team choice at creation; user defaults to viewer). A page nested under a team behaves like the team dashboard: user only.
One control, not two
On a non-team page the footer shows user primary, team as suffix — a single button opening one cascading menu instead of separate team + user pickers.

Corner cases & resolutions
Page invitee isn't on any of my teams. They appear in the menu greyed out with “not on a shared team” — selecting is disabled. Assigning them would require a team invite, which stays out of this menu's scope.
Viewer is on one team only. The team group header collapses away entirely — the menu looks exactly like today's user-assignee menu. Zero added complexity for the common case.
Task created via /task on a team-nested page. Team is the page's team, control shows user only (matches team dashboard). The “· Team” suffix never renders.
Integrated task. User half locks (as today: canAssignUser = !integration); team move remains allowed, so the suffix stays clickable and opens the menu scoped to teams.
Moving teams orphans the assignee. If the current assignee isn't on the destination team, the task lands Unassigned (today's ChangeTaskTeam behavior) — the menu warns inline before confirming: “Priya isn't on Moonshot Crew — task will be unassigned.”
#archived task. Whole control is inert, matching today's canAssignTeam = !isArchived.
Rejected alternative: “teams = union of page invitees' teams.” It reads naturally but can't work — the viewer may not be a member of those teams, and ChangeTaskTeamMutation (correctly) refuses such moves. Team choice must stay scoped to viewer.teams.
